217 MacArthur Ave, Eagle Farm, QLD 4009

Our campus includes 3 buildings on a plot over 1km2. We have co-working spaces, a general work room, a classroom, a dedicated ChemLab, the Boneyard full of scavengable parts, as well as dedicated Woodworking and Metal working rooms and a giant general use warehouse.

What can you find on our Campus?

The Green Room

HSBNE is divided into 3 buildings. The first is considered our clean building, and its where you can find people working on anything from 3d printing to sewing, or maybe even combining the two. We have a large common area devoted as a general workspace and hangout zone, and we call this the green room.

We provide most of our hand tools in this room, soldering irons, oscilloscopes, laser cutters, pcb etchers, hammers, screwdrivers, drills etc.

Co-Working Desks

If you want to build in your own workspace at HSBNE, there are the coworking desks. These are limited spaces of approx 1.5m of desk, charged on top of HSBNE membership at a scaling rate, depending on demand. If you want to keep your tools at HSBNE and don't want them generally available, this is a good way to go.

The Classroom

Currently under construction, the classroom is being set up to be configurable to allow members to teach anything. It has a capacity of around 30 people.

Member Storage

We devote an entire, locked to non-members room for members to store their tools and projects in 55L tubs.

The ChemLab

The pet project of some of our members, they're building a chemistry laboratory and one day hope to get it certified.

The Boneyard

HSBNE accepts donations from businesses and our members quite regularly. We store these donations in a room known as the boneyard. If you're a member and you need to scavenge for a switch here or a motor there, the Boneyard is the place to look.

Woodworking

Our woodworking focus is nascent, but as more members join and become interested in this, we have an entire room in the warehouse devoted to it, as well as some tools: saws, dust extractors etc.

Breaker Room

Set aside for project assembly and general use, the breaker room sits between woodworking and metalworking, and houses our 1ton, 1m2 3-phase Kiln.

Metalworking

Our best equipped room, metworking holds 2 lathes, a manual mill, metal bandsaw, linisher, and welding equipment.

The Warehouse

The warehouse is our giant, general purpose room. Configurable with 2 6x4m doors and 2 6x4m concertinas, The warehouse holds our 2 ton Gantry, block-and-tackle, and our full sized car grease trap. It also is home to our Giganto-Printer, a 5x3m poster printer being converted for a new hackerspace life.

How to get to HSBNE

By Car

  • Ideal Driving Route

Make your way onto the ICB Northbound, follow through onto Kingsford Smith Drive. Take a right at Remora Rd, and a left at the first roundabout, onto MacArthur Ave. Continue past the first (and only) left hand turn (Barcham St) and turn right into our driveway just past that.

By Bus

  • Translink Planner
  • Walking Route

Currently the nearest bus stop to the space is Racecourse Road, this is about 1500m walk to the new space. If you catch a bus to Racecourse Road at Hamilton Central then from there walk south to Kingsford Smith Drive, Cross KSD at the lights and turn left, walk about 100m and turn right into Harbour Road. Follow Harbour road round past Portside shops and turn right onto Macarthur Ave at the roundabout, walk about another 900m past all the warehouses and the tank farm and the space will be on your right.

By Ferry

  • Translink Planner
  • Walking Route from Brett's Wharf
  • Walking Route from Northshore Hamilton

Most of the time the best City Cat terminal to go to is Bretts Wharf, All services going downstream to Apollo Road stop here. It's about 1500m walk to the new space. If you catch a City Cat to Brett's Wharf walk up the ramp and turn right onto Kingsford Smith Drive, walk about 300m and turn right into Harbour Road. Follow Harbour road round past Portside shops and turn right onto Macarthur Ave at the roundabout, walk about another 900m past all the warehouses and the tank farm and the space will be on your right.

Some services, mostly on weekends and during peak hour, go to the Northshore Hamilton City Cat terminal, If you happen to get off here. Walk up the ramp and through the park, turn left and walk about 600m past the really TALL shed and car park and the space is the first driveway on your left.

Walking / Riding

Shortest most direct route. 7.9Km This is the shortest quickest way there from the city, lots of traffic, pedestrians and 99% main roads. I would only ride this if you are happy riding on the road in mains road traffic conditions.

Safest cycling route. 10.1Km This route takes you round the back of the city and mostly stays to bike paths or bike lanes, it is a bit hillier but there is a lot less traffic to deal with.

The scenic route. 15.3Km This is a reasonably long and mostly flat ride, there are a few hills when you come round past the story bridge including one evilly steep one, but it's well worth doing. Wear plenty of sunscreen and bring water to drink as there's a few gaps with little cover and no water to be found.
